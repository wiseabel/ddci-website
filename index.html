Greening Uganda's Cattle Corridor

The Dry Cattle Corridor Initiative (DCCI) partners with farmers and communities to restore degraded landscapes across the cattle corridor — improving soil, water retention, and livelihoods through targeted reforestation and sustainable land management.

Our Work Get Involved

The Problem

Overgrazing, drought, and deforestation have reduced pasture and water sources in districts such as Kiruhura, Lyantonde, Ssembabule, Nakaseke, Luweero, and Nakasongola.

Our Approach

We combine community mobilization, farmer training, seedling distribution, and monitoring to ensure long-term survival and benefits.

Impact

Improved fodder availability, stabilized soils, enhanced household incomes, and stronger climate resilience.

Recommended Tree Species

The following species are recommended for the corridor (selection depends on local site conditions):

  • Grevillea robusta
  • Calliandra calothyrsus
  • Leucaena leucocephala
  • Terminalia schimperiana
  • Acacia species (eg. Acacia senegal)
  • Ficus natalensis
  • Albizia species
